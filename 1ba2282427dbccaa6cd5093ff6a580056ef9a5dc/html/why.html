Those who are passionate about education have the same goal in mind - support student success and graduation rates. However, new models of education have shifted roles and responsibilities that have traditionally belonged to one educator to groups throughout the university. This results in unclear roles and new responsibilities that defy hundreds of years of teaching tradition.

Loopâ€™s publishing workflow addresses this challenge by serving course instructors and course developers across departments. Data-driven quizzes can be included in course study guides or delivered directly to students with specific needs, supporting multiple teaching styles. Loop includes collaborative quiz creation, a data rich question bank, and customizable options for distributing quizzes to students.

Critical features have emerged from extensive research in learning science, technical tools and online education. As a result, Loop aims to

  • bring curricular elements into shared spaces in order to reduce redundancy
  • facilitate collaboration between course designers and instructors
  • encourage ownership and accountability for assessment design
  • improve learning outcomes for our students establish a dialogue about effectiveness of learning content
